Your responsibilities in using the information contained the background check can be found at http://www.ftc.gov/os/statutes/fcrajump.shtm. The FCRA governs the use of reports obtained from a consumer reporting agency.

If there is negative data in a Background Check you receive, and you choose to take "adverse action" (i.e. choose to not hire that individual due to information in the Background Check), the FCRA requires you to take certain procedural steps, which can be found at http://www.ftc.gov/os/statutes/fcrajump.shtm. These include but are not necessarily limited to: providing pre-adverse notice to the individual who is subject to the Background Check report (Driver), suspend the hiring process for a period of time for the Driver to dispute the information with the consumer reporting agency and notifying the Driver who is the subject of the report of your decision to take adverse action based on information contained in the Background Check report.
